The reframe
IFS is not only a method of therapy.
It is a lens and a language.

Therapy is just one application. We open IFS as a way of self-awareness — for relationships, for work, and for the way you live with yourself. We drop the frame "this is only for psychologists" from minute one.

Why this matters — for everyone

There are no bad parts.

When we want to change, we lean on the part that's for it — and quietly silence the parts that doubt or fear. So an inner battle begins: you know what you "should" do, yet you put it off, avoid, sabotage.

IFS offers another view: even the most "irrational" behaviour comes from a part that has been trying to protect you. Nothing inside you is the enemy.

Guthrie's approach
Evolving IFS
Classic IFS · bottom-up

Part by part

Each part meets Self; slowly the constraints release and access to Self grows. Healing from the ground up.

Evolving IFS · top-down

Self first

Go directly to Self before working with parts. From that grounded presence, everything else becomes possible — far beyond the therapy room.

The model is not static — it is ever-evolving. This is what makes Guthrie's work distinctive, and what makes it land for everyone.
